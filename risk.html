Avalanche risk modelling (Valais, Switzerland)

The objective of this academic project was to analyze the risk of avalanches in the Swiss Alps and identify the towns and villages that were most at risk. For the purpose of this project, it was assumed that the authorities of the Valais canton had approached a GIS expert to deliver an analysis that prioritized the towns and villages that should be targeted for risk mitigation measures.

Given the limited resources for preventing avalanches, it was essential to identify the areas with the highest risk and take necessary measures to minimize the potential damage. To achieve this objective, the project utilized open spatial data and tools such as topographic functions, buffer zones, zonal statistics, and simple spatial analyses. The risk modeling was critical as it provided a systematic approach to identifying and assessing the risks associated with avalanches. GIS was used to integrate and analyze various spatial datasets to create a risk map that identified the high-risk areas and towns/villages that were most under the risk of confronting avalanches.

The risk index was used to rank the towns/villages based on the severity of the risk. The project identified Agarn as the town with the highest risk index of 45, indicating that it was at the greatest risk of experiencing avalanche events. To mitigate the risk, the town could consider implementing measures such as snow barriers, deflection walls, and snow sheds.

The project's conclusions demonstrated the potential for cost-effective and data-driven decision-making in risk management. By utilizing open spatial data and GIS tools, the project provided valuable insights into the risk of avalanches in the Swiss Alps and offered a framework for prioritizing risk management measures. The risk modeling approach helped demonstrate how authorities could allocate the limited resources available more effectively and take measures to reduce the potential damage of an avalanche event.
